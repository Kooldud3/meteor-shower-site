What Are Meteors?

Meteors are small fragments from comets or asteroids that light up the sky when they enter the Earth's atmosphere. They burn up due to friction, creating the beautiful streaks of light we often call "shooting stars."

How to Watch Meteor Showers

  • Find a dark spot away from city lights.
  • Allow your eyes to adjust to the dark for about 20 minutes.
  • Use a blanket or a reclining chair to get comfortable while looking at the sky.
  • Dress warmly, especially during colder months.
  • Check the weather forecast to ensure clear skies.
